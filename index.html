The challenge

Local villages are under pressure.

01

Foot traffic is declining

Spending is shifting online and to large retail centres. Each shop fights this battle alone, without a shared system working in their favour.

02

Customers have no reason to stay

Once a customer's immediate need is met, they leave. There's nothing in the village ecosystem pulling them next door.

03

Ten shops, ten marketing efforts

Every trader is promoting separately — different channels, different messages, different budgets. The village has no collective voice.

04

Local spending leaks out daily

Without any retention infrastructure, money earned in the village is spent elsewhere. The strip has no economic layer holding it together.

How it works

Simple by design.

No app to download. No POS system to change. No staff training sessions. Just a small QR code on the counter and a system that quietly does its work.

01
🛍

Customer shops locally

Any purchase at any participating shop on the strip qualifies.

02
⬡

Staff confirm the spend

The trader enters the purchase amount on their device. One step, ten seconds.

03
◎

Customer earns Village Points

Points land on their phone instantly. Their balance grows with every visit.

04
↩

Spend anywhere in the village

Points redeem at any participating shop — sending customers back along the strip.

Two devices, one transaction. The customer shows their QR code — you enter the amount. That's the entire interaction. The same familiar pattern as tap-to-pay, but the value stays in the village.

Why Village Rewards

Not a loyalty app.
An economic layer.

01

Precinct-first, not merchant-first

We activate villages — not individual shops. Onboarding means 5–15 traders at once. The village is the product, not the app.

02

Shared village currency

Points earn and spend across the whole strip — not locked to a single store. This drives cross-shop behaviour by design, not by chance.

03

Always-on infrastructure

Not a campaign. Not a promotion during construction works. A continuous system that runs quietly behind every transaction.

04

Web-first, zero friction

No app to download. No card to carry. A QR scan is the complete customer onboarding journey — under ten seconds start to finish.

05

Village identity built in

Each village has its own page, its own campaigns, its own story. This isn't generic SaaS — it's infrastructure with a sense of place.

06

A foundation, not a ceiling

Local spending contributing to ecological projects. Village data for council decisions. A civic layer for communities that want more than just points.
